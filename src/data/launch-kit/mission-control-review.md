# Mission Control Review Checklist

Babysitting is sitting there watching every step and doing half the work yourself. Orchestrating is letting the Personas do the work and checking the results on your terms. Mission Control is where you do the second one — it's the cockpit where you see what your agents did, what's waiting on you, and what it cost. Here's how to read each part.

## The six summary tiles

The row of tiles across the top is your at-a-glance status: what ran, what's in progress, what needs you, what got approved, and what's spent. Look at these first. If a number looks off — more waiting on you than usual, something that didn't run — that's your cue to open the section underneath it.

## The "active right now" pane

This shows what the agents are working on this moment. You don't have to do anything here except glance at it. It tells you the office is running. If something's been sitting "active" far longer than that kind of work should take, open it and see where it's stuck.

## The "staged for approval" queue

This is the important one. Every draft — emails, follow-ups, outreach, anything the agents produced — lands here and waits for you. Nothing goes out until you approve it. Read each one like you're reading your own draft: if it's right, approve it and it sends. If it's close, edit it first. If it's wrong, reject it and the agent learns. This queue is the line between you and your customers, so don't approve on autopilot.

## The Cost tab

This shows what you're spending to run the office, broken down by what's using it. Check it so there are no surprises. If one Persona or App is costing more than it's worth to you, you'll see it here and can pull it back. Most owners find the cost is small next to the hours it saves — but you should know the number, not guess at it.

For the first week, read Mission Control every day. You're building trust — you want to see that the drafts are good and the spending is sane. Once you've seen enough good work to relax, a weekly review is plenty.
