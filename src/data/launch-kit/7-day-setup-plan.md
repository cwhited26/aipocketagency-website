# 7-Day AI Office Setup Plan

Work one short task a day and by the end of the week you'll have an AI office that knows your business, drafts in your voice, and runs on its own with you checking the results.

## Day 1 — Load your Business Brain

Start with what the agents need to know. Add your main offer and three core services to the Business Brain — what you sell, what's included, what it costs. This is the foundation everything else pulls from, so get it right before you move on.

Paste this into your agent:

> Here's my main offer and my three core services. Save them to my Business Brain and tell me back, in plain English, how you'd describe my business to a new customer.

## Day 2 — Set up your Admin Assistant

Turn on your first Persona. The Admin Assistant handles inbox triage, replies in your voice, logs invoices, and schedules follow-ups. Tell it how you work — your tone, what you always do, what you never do.

Paste this into your agent:

> You're my Admin Assistant. Here's how I like to handle email: my tone is [describe it], I always [your rule], and I never [your rule]. Confirm you've got it and show me how you'd reply to a typical customer question.

## Day 3 — Run your first email draft and daily brief

Now put it to work. Have your Admin Assistant draft a real email and pull together a daily brief of what needs your attention. Both come back as drafts for you to read.

Paste this into your agent:

> Draft a reply to [paste a real email or describe one], and give me a short daily brief of anything I should know about today. I'll review both before anything goes out.

## Day 4 — Connect a tool

Hook up Gmail or your Calendar so the agents can work with your real inbox and schedule. Connecting a tool is what turns drafts into things that can actually go out once you approve them.

Paste this into your agent:

> I just connected my Gmail. Pull my unread threads that look like they need a reply and draft responses for the three most important ones for me to review.

## Day 5 — Run your first lead follow-up

Chase business that's gone quiet. Point the agents at a lead or an old thread and have them draft outreach in your voice. You approve before it sends.

Paste this into your agent:

> Here's a lead I never followed up with [paste details]. Draft a follow-up in my voice that picks up where we left off and gives them a reason to reply.

## Day 6 — Your first Mission Control review

Open Mission Control and read it top to bottom. Check the summary tiles, see what the agents did, and clear the approval queue — approve the good drafts, edit the close ones, reject the wrong ones. This is the habit that makes the whole thing work.

Paste this into your agent:

> Walk me through everything you did this week and what's still waiting for my approval, so I can review it all in one place.

## Day 7 — Turn on a routine and reflect

Set one thing to run on its own. Pick a recurring job — a Monday lead sweep, a daily brief, a weekly follow-up pass — and turn it into a routine. Then look back at the week and decide what to add next.

Paste this into your agent:

> Set up a routine that drafts my daily brief every weekday morning. Then tell me which Persona or App would save me the most time to add next.
