# Mission Control Review

Babysitting is sitting there watching every step and doing half the work yourself. Orchestrating is letting the Personas do the work and checking the results on your terms. Once you're in the product, Mission Control is where you'll do the second one — the cockpit where you'll see what your agents did, what's waiting on you, and what it cost. Here's what you'll be reading once you're in.

## The six summary tiles

The row of tiles across the top will be your at-a-glance status: what ran, what's in progress, what needs you, what got approved, and what's spent. You'll look here first. When a number looks off — more waiting on you than usual, something that didn't run — that's your cue to open the section underneath it.

## The "active right now" pane

This will show what the agents are working on this moment. You won't have to do anything here except glance at it — it tells you the office is running. If something's been sitting "active" far longer than that kind of work should take, you'll open it and see where it's stuck.

## The "staged for approval" queue

This is the one you'll spend the most time in. Every draft — emails, follow-ups, outreach, anything the agents produce — will land here and wait for you. Nothing goes out until you approve it. You'll read each one like you're reading your own draft: if it's right, approve it and it sends; if it's close, edit it first; if it's wrong, reject it and the agent learns. This queue is the line between you and your customers.

## The Cost tab

This will show what you're spending to run the office, broken down by what's using it. You'll check it so there are no surprises. If one Persona or App costs more than it's worth to you, you'll see it here and pull it back. Most owners find the cost is small next to the hours it saves — but you'll know the number, not guess at it.

For your first week in the product, plan to read Mission Control every day. You'll be building trust — you want to see that the drafts are good and the spending is sane. Once you've seen enough good work to relax, a weekly review will be plenty.
