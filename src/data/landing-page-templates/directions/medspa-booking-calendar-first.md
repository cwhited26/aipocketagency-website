## Hero treatment
The visitor's path to conversion is BOOK A TREATMENT — not call, not email, not fill out a quote form. The hero centers a soft photo of a real treatment or genuine result (the brand's actual clinician working, or an actual before/after — never stock) paired with a warm headline ("Glow you've been chasing, in 45 minutes — at [Brand Name], [City]") and a primary CTA: "Book your treatment" → opens an embedded booking calendar OR scrolls to the treatment menu. A secondary "Browse treatments" link goes to the menu. Phone number is present in the header for older clients who prefer to call, but the design assumes the visitor will book online.

## Page layout
The page is built around the booking flow. Sections in order:

1. Hero with soft photo + warm headline + "Book your treatment" CTA
2. Treatment menu — 6-12 treatments with name + 1-sentence description + duration + price (transparent pricing where the brand allows) + individual "Book this" button per treatment
3. Before/after gallery — REAL client results (with consent), grouped by treatment type. Tasteful — half-face or close-up, never full-face identifying shots unless the client granted explicit permission. HIPAA-conscious cropping.
4. Meet your clinician — owner / lead provider photo with credentials, brief story, why they got into the work
5. Real client review carousel (Google Reviews / RealSelf / brand-specific)
6. Embedded booking calendar — full-width, mobile-optimized, integrates with whatever booking system the brand uses (Boulevard, Mindbody, Vagaro, etc.)
7. FAQ — questions specific to first-time med spa clients (pain levels, recovery, what to wear, can I work after)
8. Footer with location, hours, phone, parking info, Instagram handle

## Typography
- Display: a soft serif or italic accent paired with a clean sans — examples: Cormorant Garamond + Inter, Fraunces + Sora, DM Serif Display + DM Sans
- Body: clean sans (Inter, DM Sans, Sora)
- The display feels editorial / spa magazine — not corporate, not boutique
- Italic accent for a single phrase per heading ("Glow you've been chasing — *in 45 minutes*")

## Color palette
- Background: warm white / soft cream (`#FAF5F0`, `#F8F4EE`, `#FDFBF7`) — feels skincare-aisle warm
- Brand color: a single soft accent — dusty rose (`#C9A19A`), warm taupe (`#B89B82`), sage (`#A2B59F`), or warm blush (`#E8C6B0`) — never neon, never high-saturation
- Text: warm near-black (`#2C2520`) or deep brown (`#3B2F26`) — softer than pure black
- Photos provide texture and warmth

## Visual motifs
- **Soft photography** — natural light, neutral backgrounds, no over-styled studio shots
- **Embedded booking calendar** as the conversion mechanism — not a contact form
- **Per-treatment "Book this" buttons** in the treatment menu — each treatment is its own conversion path
- **Transparent pricing where the brand allows** — printing prices removes friction (brands that DON'T print prices lose conversion to those that do)
- **Real before/after gallery** with HIPAA-conscious cropping — half-face, close-up, never full identifying shots without consent
- **Meet-your-clinician section** — owner / provider photo + credentials + a warm short story
- **Italic-serif headline accent** — a soft editorial flourish that signals "premium" without screaming it
- **Instagram-embed strip** showing recent posts — the brand's social proof in motion
- **Hours + location + parking** info prominent — these are the questions clients ask

## When to use
- Med spa, aesthetic clinics, cosmetic dermatology
- High-end salons (hair, lash, brow), nail studios with a premium positioning
- Wellness clinics (IV therapy, peptide, longevity)
- Body contouring, laser treatments, injectables
- Anywhere booking is the conversion AND the brand has a real visual aesthetic to lean on
- Boutique studios where the founder / lead provider IS the brand
- Recurring-treatment clients (these brands LIVE on rebooking)

## When to NOT use
- Emergency services or trades — wrong audience, wrong register
- B2B or services where no booking is involved
- Anyone without warm, natural-light photography (this direction exposes stock photos brutally)
- Anyone whose audience is older men (the soft-feminine register won't resonate)
- Mass-market commodity beauty (Supercuts tier) — this is premium positioning

## Build complexity
MEDIUM complexity — the booking-calendar embed is the variable. Boulevard / Mindbody / Vagaro / Square Appointments each have different embed paths. Build lane should confirm which booking system the client uses BEFORE starting.
