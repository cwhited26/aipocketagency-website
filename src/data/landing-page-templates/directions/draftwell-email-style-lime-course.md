## Hero treatment
The whole page is styled as a designed marketing EMAIL: a single 640px-wide dark card (`#111111`) floating on a `#050505` page with a deep shadow and a white-5% ring. The hero fills a tall 640:820 frame with a background video, a gradient pulling the bottom 55% into the card's dark so the next section feels continuous. Brand block top-center (serif wordmark over a letterspaced "CERTIFICATES" label), a "NOW AVAILABLE" eyebrow at 0.28em tracking mid-frame, and the headline pinned to the bottom: Instrument Serif at 58px, two lines ("Learn to lead AI / and open new value"). One lime pill CTA ("Enroll Now" + arrow) in the brand's acid lime.

## Page layout
Vertical email rhythm: hero → centered intro paragraph + lime button → thin 96px divider line → serif section heading ("Transform how you lead with AI") + a rounded video card that scales 1.03 on hover → a numbered steps list (lime square badges with dark numerals, four steps) → white solid button → divider → second serif heading + video card + paragraph → a full-bleed lime CTA card (52px serif heading "Ready to lead AI at work?", dark text on lime, lime-on-dark button inverted to dark-on-lime) → an email-true footer: wordmark, partner disclaimer in 12px gray, divider, six circular social icon buttons, unsubscribe note, Support / Privacy / Terms / Unsubscribe link row, and a postal-address copyright line. Generous 76-78px side padding inside the card keeps measure tight; everything is centered.

## Typography
- Display: Instrument Serif (Google Fonts, 400 + italic) — all large headings (46-58px, line-height ~1.02), the hero wordmark
- Body: Inter (400-700) — 17-18px paragraphs at 1.55 line-height, bold button labels, letterspaced uppercase eyebrows (0.22-0.28em)

## Visual motifs
- **Email-card page** (the signature) — the entire site is one 640px column with email furniture: dividers, social row, unsubscribe note, postal address; it reads as the best newsletter the visitor ever got
- **Acid lime on near-black** — one loud accent color carrying every CTA, number badge, and the closing card
- **Numbered step rows** — 7px-radius lime squares with bold dark numerals beside 17px copy, the curriculum-as-checklist
- **Hover-zoom video cards** — rounded 14px video blocks that scale 1.03 over 700ms inside an overflow clip
- **Bottom-anchored hero headline** — brand at top, headline pinned to the gradient at the bottom of a tall video frame
- **Full-lime closing card** — the palette inverts for the final ask: dark serif on lime
- **Letterspaced eyebrows** — "NOW AVAILABLE"-style labels at 0.22-0.28em tracking

## When to use
- Course, certification, and cohort launches — the format says "announcement," not "website"
- Coaches and educators whose funnel already lives in email — the landing page matches the inbox
- Newsletter brands selling a paid product
- Single-offer pages where one narrow column focuses attention on one button
- Brands with a partner or institution credential to disclose — the footer disclaimer slot is built in

## When to NOT use
- Multi-product or multi-audience sites — one column, one offer, no navigation
- Brands wanting wide cinematic layouts (use `roamline-electric-camper-black-serif`)
- Anyone without two or three short video clips — the video cards are the proof sections
- Corporate buyers expecting a conventional site — there is no navbar at all

## Build complexity
LOW. One narrow column of stacked sections with simple hover transitions; the discipline is in spacing and the email furniture, not engineering.
