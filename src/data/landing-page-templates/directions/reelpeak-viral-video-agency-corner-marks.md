## Hero treatment
Full-screen video hero for a short-form video agency, with zero overlays or filters — the footage runs clean and all text is white on top of it. The headline does a FONT SWITCH mid-thought: line one "Agency that makes your" is Barlow font-light at 64px, line two "videos & reels viral" flips to Instrument Serif italic at the same size. The sans/serif-italic contrast is the whole personality of the page — the plain line is the setup, the italic line is the payoff.

Above the headline sits a press badge ("Featured in" + a national business magazine name) built as a double-ring liquid-glass pill: a white/10 blurred outer ring around a white/90 inner pill. Below, a max-width sub-paragraph at 75% white opacity and the CTAs. Buttons are deliberately RECTANGULAR — 2px border radius, `#f8f8f8` fill, near-black `#171717` Barlow medium text — a hard-edged counterpoint to the italic serif. The content block has 250px of bottom padding so everything floats high above the fold.

The quiet signature: four 7px solid white squares pinned exactly at the four corners of the central content container — crop marks, framing the message like a frame of footage.

## Page layout
Single hero viewport. The navbar floats fully transparent — no fill, no border strokes — with white links and a white wordmark. Hover states are restrained: nav items pick up a white/10 wash, buttons shift from `#f8f8f8` to pure white, all on smooth color transitions.

## Typography
- Display: Barlow (Google Fonts) font-light for the sans headline line, plus Instrument Serif (Google Fonts) italic for the emphasis line, both at 64px
- Body: Barlow — sub-headline at 75% white opacity, buttons in Barlow medium

## Color palette
- Text: pure white `#FFFFFF`, with 75%-opacity white for supporting copy
- Buttons and badge fill: neutral off-white `#f8f8f8`
- Button text: near-black `#171717`
- No overlay color — the video supplies all the background tone

## Visual motifs
- **Sans-to-serif-italic headline switch** — the first line in light grotesque, the punchline in italic serif at identical size; the typographic contrast carries the brand
- **Corner crop marks** — four 7px white squares at the exact corners of the hero content block, framing the copy like a viewfinder
- **Double-ring glass press badge** — a blurred white/10 outer ring around a white/90 inner pill, centered at the top as social proof
- **Sharp 2px-radius buttons** — rectangular off-white CTAs against the soft footage, shifting to pure white on hover
- **Bare transparent navigation** — no fill, no strokes, white links straight on the video
- **Unfiltered full-bleed video** — no gradient, no tint; footage chosen to hold white text on its own

## When to use
- Video and social media agencies selling short-form content
- Content studios and creator-economy brands where the reel IS the pitch
- Personal brands of editors, directors, or video strategists
- Launch pages for a single show, channel, or campaign
- Anyone with footage strong enough to run untouched behind white text

## When to NOT use
- Brands without high-grade footage — there is no overlay to hide weak video
- Corporate or compliance-heavy buyers; the italic-serif punchline reads creative-class
- Pages needing multiple sections of proof and pricing — this is one statement screen
- Logistics or industrial brands wanting hard geometry (use `targo-logistics-dark-red-clipped`)

## Build complexity
LOW complexity. One viewport, two fonts, one glass badge, four absolutely-positioned squares. A focused half-day build.
