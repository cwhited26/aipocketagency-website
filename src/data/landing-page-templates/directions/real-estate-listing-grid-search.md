## Hero treatment
The hero centers a SEARCH BAR. The visitor's path to conversion is "search a neighborhood → see listings → contact the agent." The search bar is the largest interactive element above the fold, pre-populated with the local market the brand serves (e.g. "Search homes in Knoxville, TN..."). Beside or below the search bar, the agent / team photo and a short positioning line ("Selling Knoxville for 12 years · $48M closed in 2025"). Phone number is in the header but not the visual centerpiece — the search is.

If the brand is an INDIVIDUAL agent rather than a team, the hero photo skews to the agent's professional headshot. If it's a TEAM, the hero photo is a group shot of the team.

## Page layout
Search-driven, listing-led, agent-personal. Sections in order:

1. Hero with search bar + agent/team photo + positioning line
2. Featured listings grid — 6-12 active listings with photos, beds/baths/sqft, price, neighborhood, individual "View listing" button per card. Each card opens the full listing detail (or links out to MLS / IDX feed if the broker uses one)
3. Neighborhood guides — 3-6 short market profiles of the neighborhoods the brand specializes in, each with a "See homes in [neighborhood] →" CTA back into the listing grid
4. Recent sales — 6-9 cards showing recent closed deals with photos, list/sale price, days on market — concrete proof of the agent's track record
5. About the agent / team — full bio, real story, why they do this, designations and credentials
6. Real client review carousel (Google / Zillow / RealSatisfied)
7. Contact form — but with TWO conversion paths: "I'm thinking of buying" (routes to buyer flow) and "I'm thinking of selling" (routes to a home-valuation request form). The split-CTA captures both sides of the market without a generic "contact us"
8. Footer with license number, brokerage affiliation (legally required for many state real-estate boards), social handles, office location

## Typography
- Display: an editorial serif or sophisticated sans — examples: Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, Canela (paid), Outfit (sans), DM Serif Display
- Body: clean sans (Inter, Manrope, DM Sans)
- Number emphasis (price, sqft, days on market) — `tracking-tight`, weight 600

## Color palette
- Background: white or very light gray (`#FAFAF9`, `#F9F8F6`) — keeps the focus on the listing photos
- Brand color: a single brand accent — navy is the safe bet (trust, real estate convention); deep green or burgundy work for prestige brands
- Photos provide most of the color — the palette is restrained
- Stat numbers: brand accent at brand weight

## Visual motifs
- **Search bar as the hero centerpiece** — pre-populated with the local market, autocompletes on neighborhood/city
- **Listing grid with consistent card structure** — photo, price, beds/baths/sqft, neighborhood, "View" CTA
- **Neighborhood guides** that map to the local market — establishes territory expertise
- **Recent sales carousel** — concrete proof of closed deals, not just listings
- **Agent / team photo as part of the brand**, not a footer afterthought
- **Split-CTA contact form** — buyer flow vs. seller flow
- **License number + brokerage affiliation** visible in the footer (legally required + builds trust)
- **NO video hero** — real estate visitors are browsing for listings, not movies
- **MLS / IDX integration** for active listings if the brand uses one (build lane should confirm)

## When to use
- Real estate brokers and teams (residential or commercial)
- Individual real estate agents with a personal brand
- Property managers (residential — for sale + for rent)
- Boutique real estate firms
- Brands whose business is closing transactions, not generating leads for someone else
- Markets where local pride is part of the pitch (Knoxville agent selling Knoxville, Austin agent selling Austin)

## When to NOT use
- Real estate INVESTMENT firms (different audience — investors, not homebuyers)
- Mortgage brokers (different conversion — pre-approval, not listing search)
- Real estate developers selling NEW construction at scale (use a hospitality/luxury direction)
- Property managers focused on COMMERCIAL leasing (different conversion path)
- Anyone without active listings (the listing grid is the centerpiece — empty grids kill conversion)

## Build complexity
MEDIUM-HIGH complexity — the MLS / IDX integration is the variable. iHomeFinder, Sierra Interactive, BoomTown, Real Geeks each have different embed paths. If the brand doesn't have IDX, the listing grid pulls from a manual CMS. Build lane should confirm the data source BEFORE starting.
