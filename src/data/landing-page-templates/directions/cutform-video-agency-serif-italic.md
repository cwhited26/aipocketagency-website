## Hero treatment
A single full-screen hero (min-height 90vh) over looping background video with NO color overlay — the agency's own reel quality (`/placeholder-hero.mp4`) carries the page. The type does one trick and does it hard: a centered two-line headline where line one ("Agency that makes your") is set in bold Barlow with aggressively tight tracking (-4px), and line two ("videos & reels viral") flips to Instrument Serif italic at 84px. The sans/serif voltage between the two lines IS the brand — workmanlike setup, elegant payoff.

Below the headline: an 18px Barlow Medium subtext naming the audience ("Short-form video editing for Influencers, Creators and Brands"), then a large white pill secondary CTA, "See Our Workreel", with a small play icon on the left. The primary conversion lives in the nav: a floating white bar (rounded 16px, subtle shadow) with the logo left, four 14px Barlow Medium links centered (About, Works, Services, Testimonial), and a near-black `#222` button right — "Book A Free Meeting" — featuring a 45-degree arrow inside a small circular housing.

## Page layout
One viewport, three layers: video, floating white nav card near the top, centered headline stack in the middle. Generous breathing room around every element; all text and buttons sit directly on the footage with no scrim, so legibility depends on choosing video with calm mid-tones. Fully responsive — the 84px serif line steps down on mobile and the nav collapses to logo + CTA.

## Typography
- Display: Barlow (Google Fonts, Medium/Bold) for line one at -4px tracking, plus all nav, subtext, and buttons
- Accent display: Instrument Serif (Google Fonts, italic) at 84px for the payoff line
- Subtext at 18px Barlow Medium, nav links at 14px

## Color palette

## Visual motifs
- **Sans-to-serif headline flip** (the signature) — a tight-tracked bold sans line followed by a giant serif-italic line; the contrast lands the punchline
- **Floating white nav card** — a rounded 16px white bar with soft shadow hovering over the video, holding the primary booking CTA
- **Arrow in a circle, tilted 45 degrees** — the booking button's icon sits in its own circular housing, angled up-and-right
- **Play-icon pill CTA** — a white pill "See Our Workreel" button with a small play triangle, pointing visitors at proof
- **No-overlay video confidence** — footage at full opacity with zero scrim; the work is the background
- **Audience-naming subtext** — one plain line that says exactly who this is for (influencers, creators, brands)

## When to use
- Video editing agencies, short-form content studios, post-production houses
- Creator-economy services where the reel is the pitch
- Social media agencies selling viral-format expertise
- Videographers and personal brands with a strong showreel
- Any service business whose best asset is moving footage of its own output

## When to NOT use
- Brands without high-quality reel footage — no overlay means nowhere to hide
- Copy-heavy or multi-section marketing needs; this is one screen with two CTAs
- Corporate or compliance-bound industries where "viral" framing reads wrong
- Phone-first local services (use `trades-phone-first-emergency`)

## Build complexity
LOW. One section, two fonts, a floating nav card, and standard buttons. The craft is in type sizing and picking footage that keeps white text legible.
