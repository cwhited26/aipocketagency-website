## Hero treatment
Full-screen video hero (100vh, 600px minimum) where a giant soft-serif headline in forest green does all the work. The H1 runs from 56px on mobile up to 128px on desktop, set in a warm rounded serif with tight 1.05 leading: "Crafting the / improbable" over two lines, colored `#0E7824` directly on the video with no overlay or tint. Below it, a short two-line paragraph in dark charcoal and a dark pill CTA ("Begin a venture") that deepens to pure black on hover.

The navbar is a standard flex row: geometric logo mark plus a two-weight wordmark left (bold + black weight split mid-word), five center links that turn green on hover, and a matching dark pill CTA right. Mobile collapses to a hamburger that opens a full-screen white overlay with 24px stacked links and the same pill button.

The signature is the color decision: a deep botanical green as the display color instead of black or white. Against warm footage it reads organic and assured — an agency that picked one strange, right color and committed.

## Page layout
Single full-viewport hero. Copy is left-aligned in a max-w-7xl container with padding stepping 24px → 48px → 64px across breakpoints, headline block pushed down 32-96px from the nav. The layout is plain on purpose; the serif scale and the green carry everything.

## Typography
- Display: Fraunces (substitute for the prompt's Recoleta Regular) — soft warm serif, used ONLY for the H1, 56px to 128px, leading 1.05, tight tracking
- Body: Inter (Google Fonts, 400/500/600/700) — nav, paragraph, buttons, wordmark
- The one-font-one-job rule is strict: the serif never appears outside the headline

## Color palette
- Headline green: `#0E7824` (forest/botanical — heading only, nowhere else)
- Ink: `#2D2D2F` (logo, nav links, body text, buttons)
- Button hover: `#000000`
- Mobile menu overlay: white
- No overlay on the video; the footage supplies all remaining color

## Visual motifs
- **Forest-green display serif** — the headline is the only serif and the only green on the page, at up to 128px; one color, one face, total commitment
- **Full-bleed video, no overlay** — 100% opacity footage; copy legibility comes from type weight and color, not a dark scrim
- **Two-weight wordmark** — the brand name splits weights mid-word (bold into black), a small typographic tell of craft
- **Dark pill CTA pair** — identical rounded-full charcoal buttons in nav and hero, both deepening to black on hover
- **Green hover on nav links** — charcoal links shift to the headline green, threading the accent through the chrome
- **Full-screen white mobile menu** — hamburger opens an all-white overlay with oversized stacked links, flipping the page from video-dark to paper-white

## When to use
- Creative agencies and branding studios that want personality without maximalism
- Boutique consultancies whose pitch is taste and judgment
- Sustainability, outdoor, and nature-adjacent brands (the green does double duty)
- Architecture and interiors studios with warm footage to feature
- Anyone who wants editorial confidence from a single hero, no scroll story required

## When to NOT use
- Brands whose footage clashes with green type — the headline color needs the video to cooperate
- Corporate B2B buyers expecting a sections-and-proof layout; this is a statement page (use `cognitra-ai-agency-gray-panel`)
- Dark-mode-leaning tech brands; this direction is warm and daylight
- Anyone tempted to reuse the serif in body copy — diluting the one-job rule kills the effect

## Build complexity
LOW complexity. One section, two fonts, a hamburger overlay; the entire risk is choosing footage that lets the green headline breathe.
